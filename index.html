This is my portfolio.

My name is Jonathan Zerez, and I am currently a mechanical engineering student at the Franklin W. Olin College of Engineering. I enjoy tinkering around and making cool things. I am the kind of guy who gets stuff done. Something about passion and curiosity also.


Music Mashups

A digital signal processing project in which two songs are analyzed for BPM data and then mashed together to create a new song.

The Cycloid Drawing Machine

Made with lasercut gears and interchangable linkage arms, this machine is be able to draw fully customizable cycloids

Olin Electric Motorsports-Accelerator Pedal

Designed for Olin Electric Motorsport's third electric formula SAE racecar. This new pedal has improved geometry for increased ergonomics, a new linear potentiometer, and is lighter than previous iterations.

Project Pokemon

A webscraping project who's goal was to scrape Pokemon sprite images from the internet and train a GAN to generate new pokemon sprites

8 Bit Music Maker

A webscraping project who's goal was to scrape MIDI files from the internet and train a LSTM to produce new music

Olin Robotics Lab: Simon Says

Using computer vision and an arduino, we're trying to make a 6 axis robot arm play simon says with the user. The robot can locate up to three colored buttons, generate a sequence to push them in, and record the user's attempts, turn by turn.

NewsBlind

Made for a hackathon, this project is a webapp that tries to determine if an article contains fake news or not

Flat Earth Physics

Have you ever wondered what it'd be like to throw a ball on flat earth? In this computer simulation, we modeled how a projectile would fly on a flat earth. Optimal parameters were calculated such that the projectile would come back to the launch site.

The Diddly Darn Double Jumper

Inspired by Nature's most majestic hopper: the sea cucumber, this lasercut hardboard hopper can split into two in mid-air to seemingly double jump. This project was made for a class called Design Nature
